Blast Disease

Causes

➢ All aboveground parts of the rice plant (leaves, leaf collar, culm, culm nodes, neck, and panicle) are attacked by the fungus
➢ Initial symptoms are white to gray-green lesions or spots with brown borders

Solution

✅ Seed treatment at 2.0 g/kg seed with Captan or Carbendazim or Thiram or Tricyclazole

Bacterial Leaf

Causes

➢ Water-soaked to yellowish stripes on leaf blades or starting at leaf tips with a wavy margin
➢ Leaves with undulated yellowish white or golden yellow marginal necrosis, drying of leaves back from tip and curling, leaving mid rib intact are the major symptoms.
➢ Loss in grain yield may be up to 60%

Solution

✅ Seed treatment with bleaching powder (100g/l) and zinc sulfate (2%) reduce bacterial blight.
✅ Spray fresh cowdung extract for the control of bacterial blight. Dissolve 20 g cowdung in one litre of water; allow to settle and sieve.

Sheath Blight

Causes

➢ The fungus affects the crop from tillering to heading stage.
➢ Sheath blight caused by necrotrophic fungus Rhizoctonia solani Kühn is one of the most serious diseases of rice.
➢ A yield loss of 25% was reported if the flag leaves are infected.

Solution

✅ Seed treatment with Pseudomonas fluorescens @ of 10g/kg of seed followed by seedling dip @ of 2.5 kg or products/ha dissolved in 100 litres and dipping for 30 minutes.

False smut / Lakshmi disease

Causes

➢ The disease can occur in areas with high relative humidity (>90%) and temperature ranging from 25−35 ºC
➢ Rain, high humidity, and soils with high nitrogen content also favors disease development

Solution

✅ Destruction of straw and stubble from infected plants.
✅ Avoid field activities when the plants are wet.
