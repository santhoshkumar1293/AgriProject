Food Crops

Food crops are plants grown primarily for human consumption, providing essential nutrients and energy. Examples include rice, wheat, maize, fruits, and vegetables. Their growth depends on soil fertility, moisture levels, temperature, and sunlight. Proper cultivation ensures food security, nutrition, and sustainable agriculture.

Cash Crops

Cash crops are grown primarily for sale and profit rather than personal consumption. Examples include cotton, coffee, sugarcane, tobacco, and oilseeds. Their growth depends on factors like soil fertility, moisture levels, and climate suitability. Efficient cultivation of cash crops boosts farmers' income and supports local and global economies.

Plantation Crops

Plantation crops are grown on large-scale farms for commercial purposes, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Examples include tea, coffee, rubber, coconut, and cocoa. Their growth requires well-drained soil, optimal moisture levels, and specific climatic conditions. These crops play a significant role in export earnings and the global economy.

Horticulture crops

Horticulture crops are plants grown for food, medicinal use, and aesthetic purposes. They include fruits, vegetables, nuts, herbs, flowers, and ornamental plants. These crops are cultivated in gardens, orchards, and greenhouses, often requiring specific care and climate conditions. Horticulture plays a key role in nutrition, economy, and environmental sustainability.
